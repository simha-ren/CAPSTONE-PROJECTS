Medecins Sans Frontieres-Field Operations data platform

MSF runs humanitarian operations in 70+ countries.
They want to build a unified data platform that consolidates field clinincl data
patients intake forms,drug inventory logs,outbreak reports and staff deployment
records-from 400 field sites into a central analytics hub for their Geneva headquaters team.

Recommend approach : ELT(Snowflake+dbt)
For MSF'S field operation data paltform, the shopify-style ELT pattern is the better fir over ETL.

1.Infrastructure Constraints --> Batch-friendly
  . Field sites upload daily CSV dumps(no real time)
  . ELT works well with batch ingestion+post-load transformation
  . ETL with streaming is unnecessary overhead

2.Data varability --> need transformations
  . RAW data is stored first
  . Transformations happen later using dbt models, which analysts can update easily

3.Sensitive data
  . Patients data includes names,diagnoses,GPS locations must be anonymized
  . ELT uses staging layer:
         Raw Zone(restricted access)
         Clean layer(analytics layer)
      keeps replayability + audit trail, while forcing privacy controls

4. Analytics requirements
   . Analysts can run models without re-ingesting data
   . supports exploratory analytics and evoloving questions

5.Latency tolerance
  . No need for expensive real-time systems
  . Batch processing is cost-efficient

6. Cost Efficiency
   . Cheap storage
   . on-demand .compute
-----> Important for NGO'S like MSF with budget sensitivity

ETL IS NOT FIT Because:
. High infra cost
. Fixed transformation logic
. Real-time streaming focus
. Inflexible analytics

We choose ELT because it is flexible,cost-effiecient,analyst-friendly.sufficient for latency requirements.
